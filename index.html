01 — Project Overview

The Platform at a Glance

What is Market IQ?

Market IQ is an intelligent real estate platform designed to unify fragmented property data, streamline lead management, and deliver actionable insights for agents, investors, and homebuyers — consolidating listings, CRM, analytics, and communication into a single cohesive experience.

Design Motivation

Real estate professionals juggle 5–7 disconnected tools daily. The motivation was to create a unified platform that reduces cognitive load, eliminates context-switching, and surfaces the right data at the right time. As the sole senior UX designer, I owned every decision from research through validated prototype.

07 — Design System

Scaling Consistency

Systematic visual language across 40+ screens.

Fig 7. Market IQ UI Design System

Color Tokens

8 semantic tokens. Primary tested for WCAG AA. Neutral palette for hierarchy.

Typography Scale

Inter for UI, JetBrains Mono for data. 6-step scale for scanning vs reading.

Component Library

45+ components: buttons, inputs, cards, modals, nav. All states documented.

Annotation

I built the entire design system myself in Figma with auto-layout and variants. As a solo designer, it was essential: eliminated inconsistency across 40+ screens. Naming convention mapped Figma→code 1:1, reducing handoff ambiguity by ~40%.

11 — Reflection

What I Learned

As sole senior UX designer, I carried Market IQ from ambiguous problem space to validated prototype with measurable business impact — every finding, flow, wireframe, component, and test session was my work.

My Solo Ownership — End to End

Design

Wireframes, system, hi-fi — all me

Testing

Plan, moderation, analysis — all me

Key Takeaways

  • Flows before screens. Mapping flows first prevented isolated features.
  • Lo-fi invites honesty. Rough wireframes = layout critique; polished = color critique.
  • Test on real devices. Mobile issues only surfaced on actual touch.
  • Solo = self-discipline. I built in structured self-critique to stay objective.
  • Design drives revenue. The $2.4M result proves UX is a business investment, not a cost.

What I'd Do Differently

  • Earlier a11y audit. Integrate WCAG during wireframing.
  • More exploratory concepts. Try 2–3 IA alternatives in lo-fi.
  • Quant baseline. Pre-redesign metrics for rigorous comparison.
  • Critique partner. Weekly crit even if not on the project.
