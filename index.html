01 — Project Overview

The Platform at a Glance

Fig 1. Market IQ dashboard with KPIs, revenue trends, lead sources, and deal pipeline

What is Market IQ?

An intelligent real estate platform that unifies fragmented property data, streamlines lead management, and delivers actionable insights for agents, investors, and homebuyers — consolidating listings, CRM, analytics, and communication into one experience.

What I Designed

  • Research plan: 12 interviews, 200+ survey respondents, competitive analysis of 8 platforms
  • 3 personas with goals, pain points, and behavioral patterns from affinity mapping
  • 2 navigation flows: Property Search (7 steps) and Lead Management (6 steps)
  • Lo-fi wireframes for 5 core modules with annotated layouts

Motivation

Real estate professionals juggle 5–7 disconnected tools daily. The goal was a unified platform that reduces cognitive load, eliminates context-switching, and surfaces the right data at the right time.

06 — Lo-Fi Wireframes

Structuring Before Styling

Layout, hierarchy, and content structure — deliberately avoiding visual design to keep feedback grounded in UX logic.

Design Decision

Created in Figma at low fidelity — grayscale, placeholder images. Simplicity kept stakeholder reviews focused on IA rather than visual details. I walked through flows as each persona after every pass; 2 informal agent walkthroughs revealed that agents needed quicker pipeline access from the dashboard ("Active Deals" card), and buyers wanted the schedule CTA above the fold on property details.

07 — Design System

Scaling Consistency

Systematic visual language ensuring consistency across 40+ screens.

Fig 7. Market IQ design system — colors, buttons, inputs, cards, and typography

Color Tokens

8 semantic tokens with variants. Primary tested for WCAG AA contrast on both light and dark backgrounds.

Typography Scale

Inter for UI, JetBrains Mono for data. 6-step scale with heights optimized for scanning vs. reading.

Component Library

45+ components with auto-layout and variants: buttons, inputs, cards, modals, navigation. All states documented.

Design Decision

Built in Figma with auto-layout and variants. Each component includes usage guidelines, spacing rules, and accessibility notes. The naming convention maps Figma components to code 1:1, reducing design-to-dev handoff questions by approximately 40%.

Positive Findings

  • Dashboard intuitive — all participants found key metrics within 10 seconds
  • Property search: 100% success rate across all segments
  • Pipeline drag-drop described as "satisfying" by 8/12
  • Schedule flow averaged 45 seconds (under 2-min target)

Issues & Iterations

  • Filter panel hidden on mobile

    2/4 didn't find toggle. Fix: persistent filter pill bar.

  • Lead source labels unclear

    3 confused "Referral"/"Organic". Fix: icons + tooltips.

  • No property comparison

    4/12 tried multiple tabs. Fix: "Add to Compare" side-by-side.

Methodology

Designed the test plan, recruited 12 participants across 3 segments, moderated all 60-minute sessions, and analyzed results via affinity mapping. Used think-aloud protocol with task-based scenarios and standardized scripts to avoid moderator bias. SUS 87.8/100 places the design in the "Excellent" range (95th percentile). All 3 issues were addressed in a second design iteration before engineering handoff.

11 — Reflection

What I Learned

Key Takeaways

  • Flows before screens. Mapping navigation flows first prevented designing isolated features that don't connect.
  • Lo-fi invites honesty. Rough wireframes generate layout critique; polished comps generate color critique.
  • Test on real devices. Mobile usability issues only surfaced when users touched actual screens.
  • Standardized scripts prevent bias. When you're both designer and researcher, neutral prompts are essential.

What I'd Do Differently

  • Earlier accessibility audit. Integrate WCAG checks during wireframing, not after.
  • More exploratory concepts. Try 2–3 IA alternatives in lo-fi before committing.
  • Quantitative baseline. Measure pre-redesign task metrics for rigorous comparison.
  • Design critique partner. Weekly crit sessions even with someone outside the project.
